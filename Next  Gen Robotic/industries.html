Robotics in Healthcare

Healthcare robotics is one of the most impactful applications of modern robotic technology. From performing complex surgeries to dispensing medication and supporting rehabilitation, robots are improving patient outcomes across the globe.

  • Surgical Robots
    • da Vinci Surgical System enables minimally invasive surgery
    • Smaller incisions lead to faster patient recovery times
    • Surgeon controls robot arms from a console with full precision
  • Rehabilitation Robots
    • Exoskeletons assist patients with mobility impairments
    • Robotic therapy helps stroke patients regain movement
  • Pharmacy Robots
    • Automated dispensing reduces medication errors significantly
    • Speeds up prescription fulfilment in hospitals and pharmacies

Over 5,000 da Vinci Surgical Systems are currently in use globally, having performed over 10 million procedures (Intuitive Surgical, 2023).

Robotics in Manufacturing

Manufacturing was the first sector to widely adopt robotics. Industrial robots have transformed production lines by increasing speed, precision, and safety while reducing costs associated with human labour in repetitive or hazardous tasks.

  • Automotive Industry
    • Robotic arms weld and assemble car bodies at high speed
    • Paint robots apply uniform coatings with minimal waste
    • Quality inspection systems identify defects instantly
  • Electronics Manufacturing
    • Micro-precision robots assemble circuit boards
    • Collaborative robots work safely alongside human workers

Robotics in Logistics

The logistics and warehousing sector has seen dramatic improvements from robotic automation. E-commerce growth has accelerated the adoption of robots to handle the enormous increase in order volumes efficiently and accurately.

  • Warehouse Automation
    • Amazon Kiva robots move shelves directly to pickers
    • Sorting robots classify and route parcels at high speed
    • Autonomous mobile robots navigate warehouse floors safely
  • Delivery Robots
    • Last-mile delivery robots operate on pavements in pilot cities
    • Drone deliveries reduce delivery times in rural areas

Robotics in Agriculture

Agricultural robotics addresses critical challenges including labour shortages, food security, and sustainability. Precision robots apply only what is needed, where it is needed, reducing chemical use and environmental impact.

  • Crop Management
    • Harvesting robots pick fruit and vegetables gently and quickly
    • Weeding robots identify and remove weeds without herbicides
    • Soil sampling robots analyse nutrients and moisture levels
  • Aerial Monitoring
    • Agricultural drones survey large fields from the air
    • Thermal imaging identifies diseased crops early

Robots in General Environments

Beyond specialised industries, robots are increasingly present in public and domestic spaces, from schools and hotels to disaster response teams and space exploration missions.

  • Education
    • Educational robots teach coding and problem-solving to students
    • AI tutors personalise learning experiences for individuals
  • Emergency Response
    • Search and rescue robots enter collapsed buildings safely
    • Bomb disposal robots neutralise explosive threats remotely
  • Space Exploration
    • NASA's Perseverance rover explores the Martian surface
    • Robotic arms on the ISS perform external maintenance tasks
