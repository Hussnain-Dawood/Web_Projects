Types of Robots

Explore the different categories of robots driving change across industries, from industrial arms to humanoid companions.

  Fundamentals

What is a Robot?

A robot is an automated machine capable of performing tasks either autonomously or under human control. Modern robots integrate mechanical systems, sensors, actuators, and computational intelligence to interact with their environment.

Key components common to most robots include a physical structure or chassis, sensors that perceive the environment, a processing unit that makes decisions, actuators that enable movement, and a power source.

The word "robot" originates from the Czech word robota, meaning forced labour, first used in Karel Capek's 1920 play R.U.R. (Rossum's Universal Robots).

  Categories

Six Major Robot Types

Industrial

Industrial Robots

Used predominantly in manufacturing, these robots perform tasks such as welding, painting, assembly, and packaging. Examples include articulated arm robots used in automotive production lines by companies such as Toyota and BMW.

Service

Service Robots

Designed to assist humans in domestic or commercial environments. Examples include the iRobot Roomba for household cleaning and hotel concierge robots that guide guests. Service robots are the fastest-growing category globally.

Medical

Medical Robots

Used in surgical procedures, rehabilitation, and diagnostics. The da Vinci Surgical System allows surgeons to perform minimally invasive procedures with high precision, reducing recovery times and surgical risks for patients.

Agricultural

Agricultural Robots

Known as agribots, these machines harvest crops, monitor soil conditions, apply pesticides precisely, and tend to livestock. They address labour shortages in farming and reduce waste through precision agriculture techniques.

Autonomous

Autonomous Vehicles

Self-driving cars, delivery drones, and underwater vehicles represent a class of mobile robots capable of navigating environments without human input, using LIDAR, cameras, GPS, and machine learning algorithms.

Humanoid

Humanoid Robots

Robots designed to resemble and interact with humans. Examples include SoftBank's Pepper, used for customer service, and Boston Dynamics' Atlas, designed for search and rescue operations in hazardous environments.

  Technical Overview

How Robots Work

  • Sensing
    • Cameras and visual sensors detect objects and environments
    • LIDAR maps three-dimensional surroundings in real time
    • Force and torque sensors measure resistance and contact
  • Processing
    • Onboard computers interpret sensor data instantly
    • AI algorithms make decisions based on programmed goals
    • Machine learning allows robots to improve over time
  • Acting
    • Motors and actuators convert commands into movement
    • End effectors (grippers, tools) interact with objects
    • Feedback loops ensure accurate and controlled motion

Modern robots often use a combination of pre-programmed behaviours and adaptive AI, allowing them to handle unexpected situations in real-world environments.
